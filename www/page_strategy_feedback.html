Strategic Plan

The 2015–2019 Strategic Plan will include Victoria’s vision, mission and values as well as goals and objectives for the next five years.

I encourage everyone to engage with the development of this important document, which will guide our decision-making in the future. The first step in that process is gathering your views on what makes Victoria unique, to help develop a draft plan. 

At the recent staff forums we discussed potential areas of academic emphasis for Victoria, and what our values, culture and areas of distinctiveness may be in the future. 

Our values will define our culture and I look forward to your views on what these values may be.

I have also proposed a positioning statement—New Zealand’s globally-ranked capital city university—and I am interested in your thoughts on this and the opportunities and obligations it represents for Victoria.

Most importantly, we need to decide on areas of academic emphasis which would attract preferential investment in the years ahead. I have posed 10 ideas to stimulate thinking on what areas we should consider. Themes should stretch across faculties and disciplines ensure and both staff and students want to join our community. 

The 10 themes to prompt discussion are:

  • Inspiring better government/governance
  • Leading law
  • Empowering a trading nation
  • Cultivating creative capital
  • Shaping national identity and an equitable society
  • Driving a design-led high technology capital
  • Sustainability and our natural capital (‘smart green Victoria’)
  • Supporting the biological economy
  • Supporting health and wellbeing
  • Enabling New Zealand to strongly contribute to global challenges

Please provide any comments, views or alternative suggestions you have had by  Monday 30 June.

  Send feedback

We may publish excerpts from the feedback received, to stimulate further discussion.

Professor Grant Guilford
Vice-Chancellor
